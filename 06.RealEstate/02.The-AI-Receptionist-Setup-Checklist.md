## Product Design: "The AI Receptionist Setup Checklist"

---

### 1. What the Customer Fills In (4 Inputs)

Collected on Page 1 before any setup begins:

1. **Their existing phone setup** *(checkbox: I use my personal cell / I have a dedicated business line / I use a VoIP number like Google Voice or RingCentral / I have no separate business line yet)*
2. **Their calendar tool** *(dropdown: Calendly / Cal.com / Acuity / Google Calendar manual / None yet)*
3. **Their primary call types** *(checkbox — pick all that apply: New buyer inquiries / Seller listing calls / Open house follow-up / Referral calls / Existing client check-ins)*
4. **Their agent name, brokerage, and one "signature listing" they want the AI to reference** *(text fields — used to personalize every script the AI reads aloud)*

---

### 2. What the System Gives Them Back (Personalized Output)

Based on those 4 inputs, the customer receives:

- **A tool recommendation** — one specific tool recommendation for their phone setup (VAPI for existing VoIP / Bland.ai for cell phone / Google Voice + Zapier for those with nothing yet), with a direct link and the free tier details
- **10 AI call scripts** — pre-written word-for-word scripts the AI receptionist reads to callers, filtered to their selected call types, with their name, brokerage, and listing already inserted
- **Their calendar integration setup** — one page of numbered steps specific to the calendar tool they selected, ending with the AI booking appointments directly into their calendar without human involvement
- **3 hand-off triggers** — the exact phrases the AI listens for that immediately route the call to the live agent ("I want to make an offer," "I'm ready to list," "Can I speak to someone now?")
- **A go-live checklist** — 12 items to check before switching the AI receptionist on, in the order they need to be completed

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Framework — Page by Page

The product is **5 sections** — designed as a Notion template or fillable PDF. Each section is one page or less.

---

**Section 1: Your Setup Profile + Tool Selection** *(Page 1)*

- The 4 input fields — filled in once, referenced by every section
- A decision tree (simple visual): based on Input #1, one tool is highlighted as "Your Recommended Tool" with a link and a one-line reason why
- The single most important instruction in the guide: *"Do not move to Section 2 until your tool account is created. Takes 4 minutes."*

**This page has one job: get them to create their account before they read anything else.**

---

**Section 2: Program Your 10 Call Scripts** *(Pages 2–3)*

- The 10 scripts organized by call type (they only see the types they checked in Input #3)
- Each script is labeled (Script 1A, 1B, etc.) and runs 3–5 sentences — short enough for the AI to sound natural, specific enough to sound like a real NYC luxury agent
- Their name, brokerage, and listing are pre-filled in every script from Input #4
- A "Script Quality Check" box at the bottom: *"Read Script 1A out loud. If it sounds like you, move on. If not, change one word. Done."*

**This is where they do the core work. Everything else is configuration.**

---

**Section 3: Connect Your Calendar** *(Page 4)*

- Numbered steps specific to the calendar tool they selected in Input #2
- Ends with one verification test: *"Call your AI number. Say 'I'd like to book a call.' Confirm the appointment appears in your calendar. If yes — this section is done."*
- If they selected "None yet" — a 6-step guide to setting up a free Calendly account and connecting it, estimated time: 11 minutes

---

**Section 4: Set Your 3 Hand-Off Triggers** *(Page 5)*

- The 3 universal hot-lead phrases the AI is programmed to detect (pre-written — no customization needed)
- A simple table: Trigger phrase → What the AI does → What the agent receives (a text alert with the caller's number and the phrase they used)
- One instruction: *"Copy these 3 trigger phrases exactly as written into your tool's 'escalation' or 'transfer' settings."*

---

**Section 5: Go-Live Checklist** *(Page 6 — half page)*

A 12-item checkbox list in sequential order:

- [ ] Tool account created and phone number connected
- [ ] All 10 scripts uploaded and tested with a preview listen
- [ ] Calendar integration verified (test booking confirmed)
- [ ] 3 hand-off triggers programmed
- [ ] Business hours set (AI active / AI off)
- [ ] Voicemail fallback recorded
- [ ] Test call placed from a friend's number — AI answered, script ran, booking link sent
- [ ] Agent receives hand-off text alert on their phone
- [ ] Old voicemail greeting updated to reference AI ("Our team will respond immediately")
- [ ] StreetEasy / Zillow listing phone number updated to AI number
- [ ] Instagram bio link updated with AI booking link
- [ ] Confirm: First real lead came in and was handled without manual intervention ✓

**When the last box is checked — the system is live. The product has delivered its promise.**

---

### 4. How the Customer Gets the Result Without Reading More Than 2 Pages

**The usage path:**

**Page 1** — Fill in 4 inputs. Create the recommended tool account. (4 minutes.)

**Pages 2–3** — Copy their pre-written scripts into the tool. (15 minutes.)

**That's the system.** Every call is now handled automatically.

Pages 4–6 are configuration steps that take 20 more minutes combined — but the agent could stop after Page 3 and already have an AI answering calls with professional scripts. Pages 4–6 turn a working system into a fully automated one.

**Total time from purchase to live AI receptionist: one afternoon.**

### How to Build This With Claude

**Run this prompt to generate the 10 personalized call scripts:**

```
I'm setting up an AI receptionist for a NYC luxury real estate agent.

Agent details:
- Name + Brokerage: [INPUT #4]
- Signature listing: [INPUT #4 — listing detail]
- Call types to handle: [INPUT #3 selections]

Write 10 short AI receptionist scripts (3–5 sentences each) for these call types:
[list the selected types]

Each script must:
- Sound warm, professional, and confident — not robotic
- State the agent's name and brokerage in the first sentence
- Offer to book a call using: [INPUT #2 booking link]
- Be written for spoken audio (no bullet points, no formatting)
- End with a clear next step for the caller
- Reference [signature listing] naturally when relevant to the call type

Label each: Script 1A (New buyer inquiry), Script 2A (Seller call), etc.
```

**Run this prompt to generate the calendar integration steps:**

```
Write step-by-step numbered instructions (under 12 steps) for connecting
[INPUT #2 — their calendar tool] to [INPUT #1 recommended AI tool]
so that when a caller says "I'd like to book a call," the AI sends them
a booking link and the appointment appears automatically in the agent's calendar.

Write it for a non-technical user. No jargon. Include what they should
see on screen after each step to confirm it worked.
```

**Two prompts. Both outputs paste directly into the Notion template. Build complete.**
