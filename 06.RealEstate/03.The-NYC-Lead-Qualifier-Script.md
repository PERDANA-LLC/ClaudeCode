## Product Design: "The NYC Lead Qualifier Script"

---

### 1. What the Customer Fills In (4 Inputs)

Collected on Page 1 before running any qualification call:

1. **Their market focus and price range** *(dropdown: under $1M / $1M–$2M / $2M–$5M / $5M+ — and neighborhood focus: Manhattan / Brooklyn / Queens / Mixed)*
2. **Their primary buyer types** *(checkbox — pick all that apply: First-time buyers / Move-up buyers / Investors / Relocating executives / International buyers / Co-op only / Condo only / Mixed)*
3. **Their current showing capacity** *(number field: "I can realistically do ___ showings per week" — used to calibrate the scoring threshold)*
4. **Their assistant or team setup** *(checkbox: I run calls myself / I have an assistant who runs calls / I want to use this as a chatbot script)*

---

### 2. What the System Gives Them Back (Personalized Output)

Based on those 4 inputs, the customer receives:

- **A personalized 12-question qualification script** — questions filtered and reordered based on their buyer types and price range (a $5M+ international buyer script looks different from a $1M first-timer script)
- **A Hot / Warm / Cut scoring matrix** — each answer maps to a point value; the total score after 12 questions produces one of three outcomes with a specific next step for each
- **The 3 NYC-specific disqualifier questions** — the questions that immediately end the call (co-op board eligibility, undisclosed financial obligations, hard timeline) presented upfront so no time is wasted
- **5 "red flag" language patterns** — specific phrases callers use that signal a tire-kicker vs. a ready buyer, with instructions on what to do when each is heard
- **A "what to say next" card for each score outcome** — Hot (book showing immediately, here's the script), Warm (send to nurture, here's the message), Cut (politely end call, here's the exact language)

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Framework — Page by Page

The product is **5 sections** — one page each, designed as a Notion template, fillable PDF, or laminated call card.

---

**Section 1: Your Qualifier Profile** *(Page 1)*

- The 4 input fields — filled in once
- A visual scoring threshold: based on Input #3 (showing capacity), the system tells them their minimum score to book a showing (e.g., "You have 4 showing slots/week — only scores of 8+ get a showing")
- One instruction: *"Fill in your profile. Then go directly to the Red Flag Patterns card before your next call."*

**This page calibrates the entire system to their market and capacity.**

---

**Section 2: The 3 Instant Disqualifiers** *(Page 2 — the most valuable page)*

Three questions asked in the first 90 seconds of every call. If any answer triggers a disqualifier, the call ends politely — no further questions needed.

| Question | Disqualifier Answer | What to Say |
|---|---|---|
| "Are you currently working with another agent under a signed buyer agreement?" | Yes | "Understood — I wouldn't want to interfere with that relationship. Best of luck." |
| "Are you pre-approved for financing or purchasing cash?" | No / "Working on it" | "Happy to connect you with a lender first — here's who I recommend." |
| "What's your timeline to be in a new home?" | "Just exploring / 12+ months" | "Perfect — let me add you to our market update list and reconnect when you're closer." |

**An agent who uses only this page saves 6+ hours per week.**

---

**Section 3: The 12-Question Qualification Script** *(Pages 3–4)*

The full script, organized in 4 blocks of 3 questions each:

**Block 1 — Financial Readiness (Questions 1–3)**
Questions about pre-approval, down payment source, and co-op board eligibility. Each answer scores 0, 1, or 2 points.

**Block 2 — Timeline & Motivation (Questions 4–6)**
Questions about their move-out date, what's driving the search, and whether they've made offers before. Scores 0–2 each.

**Block 3 — NYC-Specific Readiness (Questions 7–9)**
Questions about co-op board package familiarity, board application experience, and willingness to move at NYC deal speed. Scores 0–2 each.

**Block 4 — Decision Authority (Questions 10–12)**
Questions about who else is involved in the decision, whether they've seen properties yet, and what would stop them from making an offer in the next 30 days. Scores 0–2 each.

**Maximum possible score: 24 points**
- 17–24: Hot → Book the showing
- 10–16: Warm → Send to nurture sequence
- Under 10: Cut → Politely end and add to long-tail list

---

**Section 4: The 5 Red Flag Language Patterns** *(Page 5)*

A laminate-ready card listing 5 exact phrases that signal a tire-kicker — and the scripted response to each:

1. *"We're just getting started / just doing research"* → Score -2, pivot to nurture
2. *"We want to see everything in the neighborhood before deciding"* → Ask timeline; if vague, score -2
3. *"Our current lease isn't up for 14 months"* → Automatic warm/cut depending on price range
4. *"We're also looking in [another borough / New Jersey / Westchester]"* → Score -1, probe motivation
5. *"Our financial advisor / parents / partner needs to be involved"* → Ask if they're on the call; if not, reschedule with all decision-makers present

---

**Section 5: What to Say Next — The 3 Outcome Scripts** *(Page 6 — half page)*

Three short scripts, one per score outcome:

**HOT (17–24 pts):** *"Based on what you've told me, I think we have some great options that match exactly what you're looking for. I have [day] at [time] and [day] at [time] available — which works better for you?"*

**WARM (10–16 pts):** *"You're in a great position — just not quite at the point where I want to bring you to a showing and waste your time. Let me send you [specific content] and follow up in [X weeks]. Does that work?"*

**CUT (under 10 pts):** *"I really appreciate your time. I want to be honest — based on where you are right now, I don't think a showing would be the best use of your time yet. Let me add you to our updates and we'll reconnect when the timing is right."*

---

### 4. How the Customer Gets the Result Without Reading More Than 2 Pages

**The usage path:**

**Page 1** — Fill in 4 inputs. Note your minimum showing score. Takes 2 minutes.

**Page 2** — Run the 3 instant disqualifiers at the top of every call. 80% of tire-kickers are cut here in under 90 seconds. This page alone saves most agents 5+ hours per week.

That's it for daily use. The agent never needs to read the full 12-question script during a call — Page 2 handles the majority of filtering. The full script (Pages 3–4) is used for callers who pass the disqualifiers and need deeper qualification.

**Total result: wasted showings drop to near zero after the first week of use.**
