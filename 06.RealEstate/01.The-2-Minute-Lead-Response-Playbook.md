## Product Design: "The 2-Minute Lead Response Playbook"

---

### 1. What the Customer Fills In (5 Inputs)

These are collected on a single intake page — either a Google Form, Typeform, or a fill-in section at the top of a Notion template:

1. **Their primary lead sources** *(checkbox: StreetEasy / Zillow / Open House / Instagram / Referral / Cold Call / Other)*
2. **Their market focus** *(dropdown or fill-in: Manhattan / Brooklyn / Queens / Mixed — and price range: under $1M / $1M–$3M / $3M+)*
3. **Their CRM or response tool** *(dropdown: Follow Up Boss / HubSpot / kvCORE / Gmail / iMessage / None yet)*
4. **Their booking link** *(text field: paste their Calendly or Cal.com URL)*
5. **Their agent name and brokerage** *(text fields: used to personalize every template signature)*

---

### 2. What the System Gives Them Back (Personalized Output)

Based on those 5 inputs, the system produces a **ready-to-use, personalized template library** containing:

- **30 SMS + email templates** — filtered and labeled by their specific lead sources (they only see the ones relevant to them, not all 30 generic ones)
- **Their booking link already embedded** in every template — zero editing required
- **Their name and brokerage pre-filled** in every signature line
- **CRM import instructions** — one page specific to the tool they selected in Input #3
- **A "which template to use when" quick-reference card** — one visual showing which template fires for which scenario

The customer's total editing time after filling in the 5 inputs: **under 10 minutes.**

---

### 3. Step-by-Step Framework — Page by Page

The entire product is **6 sections**, designed to be a Notion template or a clean PDF with fillable fields. No section requires more than one page.

---

**Section 1: The Promise + How to Use This in 3 Minutes** *(Page 1)*

- One paragraph explaining the only rule: *every lead gets a response within 2 minutes or you lose them*
- A visual showing the simple flow: **Lead comes in → You're unavailable → Template fires → Booking link clicked → Appointment locked**
- Instructions: *"Fill in the 5 boxes below. Then skip directly to your lead source tab."*
- The 5 input fields (inline — they fill them in right here)

**Customer reads this once. Never needs to come back to it.**

---

**Section 2: Your Personalized Template Library** *(Pages 2–5, tabbed by lead source)*

Each tab = one lead source they selected in Input #1.

Each tab contains:
- **3 SMS templates** (initial response / follow-up at 24 hrs / follow-up at 72 hrs)
- **2 email templates** (initial response / follow-up at 48 hrs)
- A **"DO NOT do this" box** — one sentence on the most common mistake agents make on that lead source (e.g., for StreetEasy: *"Don't reference the other agents they contacted. Pretend you're their only call."*)

Every template already has their name, brokerage, and Calendly link filled in from Section 1.

**This is the section they live in. Everything else is support.**

---

**Section 3: CRM Setup Instructions** *(1 Page — specific to their tool)*

- For the CRM they selected in Input #3, one page of numbered steps to load these templates as canned responses or automation triggers
- If they selected "None yet" — one page recommending the fastest free setup (Gmail canned responses + iMessage shortcuts) with step-by-step instructions
- Estimated setup time displayed at the top: *"This takes 18 minutes to set up once."*

---

**Section 4: The Quick-Reference Card** *(1 Page — print or pin)*

A single visual table:

| Lead Source | First Response Template | Timing | Follow-Up |
|---|---|---|---|
| StreetEasy inquiry | SMS-1A | Immediately | Email-1B at 48 hrs |
| Open house sign-in | SMS-2A | Same evening | SMS-2B at Day 3 |
| Instagram DM | SMS-3A | Within 5 min | Email-3B at Day 2 |

Designed to be pinned above a desk or saved as a phone wallpaper. **Decision-making eliminated.**

---

**Section 5: The "If They Don't Book" Rescue Sequence** *(1 Page)*

- 3 additional templates for leads who got the initial response but didn't click the booking link
- Labeled: Day 3 / Day 7 / Day 14
- One-line instruction: *"If they haven't booked after Day 14, move them to your long-nurture list."*

---

**Section 6: REBNY Compliance Note + Done** *(Half page)*

- 3 bullet points on what NOT to include in automated responses under REBNY fair housing guidelines
- One line: *"You're set. Every lead that comes in now gets a response faster than any competitor in your market."*

---

### 4. How the Customer Gets the Result Without Reading More Than 2 Pages

**The entire usage path:**

**Page 1** — Fill in 5 inputs. Read the 3-minute overview. Done with setup thinking.

**Page 2 (their lead source tab)** — Copy the SMS template for their most common lead source. Paste it into their phone or CRM. Send.

That's it. **They have a functioning 2-minute response system after touching 2 pages.**

Everything else (CRM instructions, quick-reference card, rescue sequence) is reference material they use once during setup or return to only when needed.
